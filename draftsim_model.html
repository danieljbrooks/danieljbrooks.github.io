Draftsim Model



Draft simulators are widely used training tools in the Magic: the Gathering community. Many other draft simulators have limited functionality. I built a fully featured draft simulator with smart computer opponents, pick suggestions, and automatic deckbuilding. The tool was quickly adopted by the community and is currently the leading free tool for preparing for Magic: the Gathering drafts.

The Draftsim model is designed to mimic the behavior of professional players and maximizes the quality of cards within a two color pair. Each card has a base rating and a color bonus, which scales up as more cards of that color are picked. Recommended picks are shown in a table on the site, computer opponents always make the most recommended pick. Decks are automatically constructed by identifying the two colors with the best cards and playing the highest rated cards in those colors.

Draftsim has been well recieved by the community and is the leading free way to prepare for Magic: the Gathering drafts.

The valuation of cards is a sum of two components, a base rating, intrinsic to the card, and a color bias, which provides an incentive two draft cards from a two-color pair. The base ratings for each set can largely be derived from pick order lists, although some fine tuning can be done based on trends in professional draft viewers, community articles, or experience on the simulator. Generally speaking consistent cards like creatures and removal spells should be rated slightly higher and situational cards like combat tricks should be rated slightly lower than the professional pick order list to ensure the construction of functional decks.

To identify two open colors to play, the drafting process is broken down into two stages - the speculation stage where the model searches for two open colors and the committed stage where the model prioritizes cards of those colors.

Work is currently underway to consider the effect of additional factors such as curve and synergy. To train these parameters, drafting data will be collected from users on the site and from user uploaded drafts played on the official Magic Online client. This data can be used to train more sophisticated drafting models, allow users to play against a variety of opponents, and provide insights into the drafting stratgies employed by a wide range of users.
