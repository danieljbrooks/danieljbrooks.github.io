Draftsim Model



Draft simulators are widely used training tools in the Magic: the Gathering community. Many other draft simulators have limited functionality. I built a fully featured draft simulator with smart computer opponents, pick suggestions, and automatic deckbuilding. The tool was quickly adopted by the community and eventually sold.

The Draftsim model maximizes the quality of cards within a two color pair. Each card has a base rating and a color bonus, which scales up as more cards of that color are picked. Recommended picks are shown in a table on the site, computer opponents always make the most recommended pick. Decks are automatically constructed by identifying the two colors with the most good cards and playing the highest rated cards in those colors.

This will be a figure showing the draftsim model.

In the future, there is an interest in considering additional factors such as curve and synergy. To train these parameters, draft logs from official online MTGO tournaments could be collected, cleaned, and used to train the updated model This would allow for a more refined drafting model and could be useful for community members who wish to practice against a variety of strategies.
